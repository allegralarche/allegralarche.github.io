Summer 2014
The Sports Quotient: Programming Intern

This past summer I worked at a small sports media website that was looking to migrate their content-heavy site form wordpress to a faster and more easily customizable platform. I had never worked with Django before, but my coworker had some experience with it and suggested we use it for the new site. I was quickly brought up to speed, and the two of us worked together to completely rebuild and revamp the site from scratch over the ten weeks we worked there. In addition to the migration, we added a new tracking system for editors and writers in the backend to help everyone stay on top of their work, a more intricate quiz system, and a generally more friendly user interface for readers. I think I learned more about web development and design over the course of this summer than I have over the course of my entire programming career!

Summer 2013
Internal Drive Tech Camps:Instructor

The summer after my freshman year, I worked at a camp near my home teaching kids aged 9-12 basic programming concepts. The course used the game of Minecraft and its intricate built-in command block system to demonstrate concepts like logic flow and loops to the campers. The task for their final projects was to make a multi- stage game using the skills they learned in the course that could be played by their peers within Minecraft. This was a beyond- rewarding experience for me; I was constantly in awe of how much these kids were able to learn and accomplish in just one week.
